Explore Rome and Sorrento

The Rialto Bridge is a famous bridge built in 1588 that replaced previous wood bridges that occupied the same location. The design of the bridge shows an example of Venetian Renaissance architecture. The Rialto Bridge is famously one of the most popular tourist places in Venice. It has also been seen in numerous films located in the heart of Venice.

Doge’s Palace, built in 1424, belonged to former Vatican Republic is one of the most tourist filled sights in Venice. The ceiling are covered in gold and artwork from Tintoretto’s. The most famous rooms inside Doge’s palace are The Bridge of Sighs, The Great Council Chamber, and The Golden Staircase.

The David is one of the most historical statues in Italy. The David originally was built for the roof of Opera del Duomo, but was the put inside the Galleria dell ’Accademia to protect it from getting destroyed. This piece was created by Michelangelo between 1501 and 1504 during the Italian Renaissance. The David represents a hero who defeated Goliath.

The Duomo is one of the most attractive landmarks In Florence. It began being built in 1296 and wasn’t consecrated until 1436. In front of the Duomo is the Baptistry of San Giovanni which is one of the oldest buildings in Florence. It represents artistic and architectural attainment in Italy, with sculptures by Donatello and Andrea del Verrocchio
